# Discovery call transcript — Acme Tools onboarding

**Date:** 2026-06-18
**Present:** Jordan Lee (Head of Operations, Acme Tools) · Riley Chen (founder, Northstar Ops)

---

**Riley:** Thanks for making time, Jordan. Before we dig in — what made you reach out now?

**Jordan:** Honestly, onboarding is killing us. We sell a B2B inventory tool, and every new
customer takes my team about three weeks of hand-holding before they're live. We onboarded
eleven accounts last quarter and it nearly broke my two ops people. We've got fourteen more
signed for Q3 and there's no way we survive that at the current pace.

**Riley:** Three weeks per account — what does that look like day to day?

**Jordan:** It's all manual. We rebuild the same setup checklist in Notion every time, we
get on three or four calls per customer to import their data, and there's no standard
playbook so it depends who's running it. New customers churn in the first 60 days when
onboarding drags — we lost two accounts last quarter that way. Each account is worth about
$2,000 a month to us, so a churned account is real money.

**Riley:** Got it. If you waved a wand, what does success look like?

**Jordan:** Onboarding down to one week, mostly self-serve, with a repeatable playbook my
team runs the same way every time. I don't need it fully automated — I need it *systematized*
so it's not living in my head.

**Riley:** That's very doable. Is this a project you're looking to start soon?

**Jordan:** Yeah — those fourteen Q3 accounts start landing mid-July, so I'd want a playbook
in hand before then. Call it four weeks out.

**Riley:** And in terms of investment — do you have a budget range in mind for this?

**Jordan:** We haven't set a hard number. For something that saves my team this much pain I
could see spending somewhere in the low five figures. I'd need to be the one to greenlight
it but I own this budget, I don't need sign-off from above.

**Riley:** Helpful. One more — are you talking to other folks about this, or evaluating
building it internally?

**Jordan:** We tried building it internally and it stalled twice — nobody owns it. I'd rather
bring in someone who's done this. You're the only outside person I'm talking to right now.

**Riley:** Perfect. Let me put together a proposal with a couple of options and send it over
this week.

**Jordan:** Sounds great. The sooner the better — those accounts aren't waiting.
