# Proposal: Cut Acme's onboarding from 3 weeks to 1 — a playbook your team runs the same way every time

Prepared for **Jordan Lee, Acme Tools** · by **Riley Chen, Northstar Ops**

## The problem we discussed

Onboarding is your bottleneck. Every new customer takes ~3 weeks of manual hand-holding —
rebuilt checklists, 3–4 data-import calls, and no standard playbook, so quality depends on
who's running it. You onboarded 11 accounts last quarter and it nearly broke your two-person
ops team. With 14 more landing mid-July, the current pace doesn't survive Q3 — and slow
onboarding already cost you two accounts to first-60-day churn at ~$2,000/mo each.

## What success looks like

Onboarding compressed to **~1 week, mostly self-serve**, driven by a **repeatable playbook**
your team runs identically every time — systematized out of your head, not dependent on any
one person. (Objectives, not guarantees.)

## Scope

**Included**
- End-to-end onboarding playbook (standard operating procedure + decision points)
- Self-serve customer setup checklist + data-import guide that removes 2 of the ~4 calls
- Reusable templates (welcome, kickoff, go-live) and an internal handoff runbook
- One live training session for your ops team on running the playbook

**Not included** (available as add-ons)
- Building software/automation to fully automate onboarding
- Ongoing managed onboarding delivery
- CRM or tooling migration

## Approach

1. Audit current onboarding (shadow 1–2 live accounts, pull your Notion checklist).
2. Map the critical path and cut the steps that don't move customers to "live."
3. Draft the playbook + self-serve assets; pressure-test against a real upcoming account.
4. Train your ops team and hand off the runbook.
