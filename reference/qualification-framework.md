# Qualification Framework — FIT-BUST scorecard

A lightweight rubric tuned for a solo founder's reality: you have limited hours, so the
question isn't only "can this close" but "is this worth *my* one pair of hands." Score
each of the six dimensions **0–3** from evidence in the conversation. Total is out of 18.

| Dim | Name | What you're scoring | 0 | 3 |
|-----|------|---------------------|---|---|
| F | **Fit (ICP)** | Is this the kind of client/project you do well and want more of? | Off-ICP, one-off | Dead-center ICP, repeatable |
| I | **Intensity of pain** | How acute and costly is the problem in their own words? | Nice-to-have | Urgent, quantified, top priority |
| T | **Timeline** | Is there a real reason to act now? | "Someday" | Hard deadline / event-driven |
| B | **Budget** | Did a real number, range, or funding source surface? | None / "no budget" | Stated budget that fits your pricing |
| U | **aUthority** | Is your contact the decision-maker (or one call away)? | Pure researcher | Signs the check |
| S | **Scope clarity** | Can you actually define and bound the work? | Vague / shape-shifting | Crisp, bounded deliverable |

(FIT = the deal's quality; BUST = the deal's closeability. Hence FIT-BUST.)

## Reading the total

- **14–18 — Pursue.** Build the full package, recommend the Better tier, move fast.
- **9–13 — Pursue with caution.** Build the package but lead with the discovery gaps; the
  `[NEEDS CONFIRMATION]` list is your pre-proposal call agenda.
- **0–8 — Decline (or re-discover).** Don't burn hours. Either draft a gracious decline /
  referral, or propose the 3–5 questions that would move the weak dimensions.

## Scoring discipline

- Score **only** on stated evidence. No evidence for a dimension = score it **0–1** and
  add it to `[NEEDS CONFIRMATION]`. Absence of information is not a 3.
- The lowest single dimension is usually the deal's real risk — call it out explicitly as
  "biggest risk to closing," even when the total looks healthy.
- Budget of 0 with Intensity of 3 is a classic trap: high pain, no funding. Flag it.

## Red flags to surface (independent of score)

- Scope creep language ("and also, while you're at it…", "should be quick")
- Authority hedging ("I'll need to run it by…") with no named decision-maker
- Deadline with no budget (you'll be pressured to discount under time stress)
- Comparison shopping with no stated decision criteria (you're the stalking horse)
- Payment-term resistance before a number is even on the table
